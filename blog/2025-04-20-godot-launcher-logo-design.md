# Designing the Godot Launcher Logo

Hi everyone, Paulskii here and I'm one of the volunteers on the Godot Launcher project. I helped design the Godot Launcher logo and I wanted to share with you all the process of how it came to be.

## Objectives

I had three main objectives with this logo:

1. It had to be recognizable on its own
2. It had to be somewhat related to Godot
3. It had to be lighthearted but still convey a professional tone of voice

<!-- truncate -->

## Concept & Research

My initial research focused on defining what a "launcher" represents. The rocket symbol stood out as a universally recognized icon for this concept, making it a safe and solid foundation for the logo design.

## Tying in Godot

I then turned onto how I could incorporate Godot into Godot Launcher. I was inspired by the geometric logomark of the Godot logo and wondered if I could somehow build an off-shoot rocket out of it. Andrea Calabró, the visual artist behind the original Godot logo, did an incredible job of harnessing a unique feel and personality compared to other game engines.

![behind the scene logo design 01](/img/blog/images/Godot_Launcher_Logo_WIP_01.png)

## Tools and Colors

I used Adobe Illustrator for creating the logo and the rest of the deliverables (alternate colour palettes, layouts etc.) As for colours, I wanted to compliment the original Godot blue used with a striking and energetic orange foundation. I envisioned people would most likely have the Godot Engine and Godot Engine Launcher next to each other in their taskbars so I thought it would be best to see how they would look in that scenario.

## Typography

Picking the font was particularly straight-forward. I went with Nunito Sans in bold because it conveyed an extremely similar impression to the original Godot Engine font used. We wanted it to be playful but also professional next to Godot Engine.

## Iteration Process

We only went through two drafts when making the Godot Launcher logo. [Mario](/blog/authors/mariodebono) was completely onboard with the initial idea I proposed and together we thought of doing some minor tweaks for a second draft.

The first draft had the logomark appear thinner and narrower, which we changed in the second draft because of legibility at smaller scales. We also added a new primary colour base through this deep orange gradient that we thought would make the vector icon stand out a bit more. Finally, I added some minor modifications to the actual vector icon itself through the addition of the rocket wings and the exhaust flame.

![behind the scene logo design 02](/img/blog/images/Godot_Launcher_Logo_WIP_02.png)

## Final Thoughts

And that's pretty much it! Overall it was a nice challenge to go through and we think this final design does justice to the work that Mario has put into the actual product itself.
